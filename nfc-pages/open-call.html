What is this

Heritage infrastructure
built for the open world

ARCHAI connects physical tags — NFC chips, QR codes, stickers, printed posters — to living conversations with cultural objects. Every tag links to a visitor page: a story, an image, an AI voice that speaks in first person as the object itself.

Visitors can ask questions, leave responses, and hear the work read aloud. Their words join the collection record. The conversation keeps growing.

We are now inviting curators, curatorial students, and street artists around the world to take this infrastructure into public space — galleries, streets, markets, schools, warehouses, underpasses, fences.

The collection is open. The posters are free. The streets are yours.

Process

How it works — five steps

01 ·· FIND

Choose an object

Browse the ARCHAI collection index. Objects from the Met, Tate, Rijksmuseum, Cleveland, Smithsonian, and street art archives across Vancouver, Brussels, and New York — all available now, with more added regularly.

→
02 ·· DOWNLOAD

Download your poster

Every visitor page has a Download Poster button. Choose your format: sticker (100mm sq), postcard (A6), A4, A2, or A0. Each includes the object image, title, artist, institution, a description excerpt, and a QR code that links directly back to the live ARCHAI page.

→
03 ·· PRINT

Print at any scale

Stickers and postcards print beautifully at home. A4 for zines and notice boards. A2 for gallery prints and paste-ups. A0 for large-format outdoor installations. Any print shop can handle these files — the QR code is sized to scan reliably at every format.

→
04 ·· DEPLOY

Put it anywhere

Gallery wall. Street corner. Market stall. Community noticeboard. The back of a bus seat. Your studio window. Wherever someone might stop, look, and reach for their phone.

→
05 ·· CONNECT

People scan, the object speaks

Whoever scans meets the full ARCHAI visitor page: story, image, AI conversation, voice playback, and visitor response. Their words join the collection record. The object now has a presence in your city.

The poster

What you download — what gets printed

Each poster is a high-resolution PNG rendered directly in your browser — no server, no upload, no account. ARCHAI branding, the object image, metadata, and a unique QR code that links to the live visitor page for that exact object.

  • Sticker 1181 × 1181 px · 300 dpi · 100mm sq Sticker sheet · Paste-up · Zine insert
  • Postcard 1748 × 1240 px · 300 dpi · A6 Mail · Hand out · Gallery takeaway
  • A4 2480 × 3508 px · 300 dpi Home print · Notice board · Framing
  • A2 2480 × 3508 px · 150 dpi Gallery wall · Paste-up

The scan experience

What happens when someone
points their camera

They land on a full ARCHAI visitor page. No app download. No account. Just open the camera, scan, and meet the object.

01 ··

"Tell me your history." The object speaks in first person — 3–4 sentences, warm and clear, from its own record.

02 ··

"What are you made of?" The visitor types or speaks any question. The AI answers from verified metadata.

03 ··

Voice playback reads the reply aloud. No screen needed. Works in the street, in a market, in the dark.

04 ··

Visitors leave a response. Their words join the collection record alongside centuries of curatorial notes.

05 ··

Related objects appear below. One scan can open a thread through Tate, the Rijksmuseum, and a mural in Vancouver.

06 ··

All processing is local. No data leaves to a cloud. Sovereignty of collection data is preserved end to end.

Join the open call

Submit your work &
get featured

Show us where the collection went. We feature documented deployments here — with the story of how they came to be, and the conversations they sparked.

01 · Tag

Share on social

Post your deployment on any platform and tag it #ARCHAIinTheWild. Include the object title and where you put it. We monitor the tag and will reach out to feature your work.

02 · Email

Direct submission

Send documentation — photos of the deployment, the object page link, a note on the context — to open@archai.io. We'll contact you about featuring it and may include it in ARCHAI research outputs.

03 · Contribute

Add your own work

Street artists: if you want your own practice included in the ARCHAI collection — not just deploying others' work but having your work become a scannable object in the archive — get in touch. We're building an open onboarding pathway for living artists.

04 · Open source

Build with ARCHAI

ARCHAI is open-source infrastructure. Institutions, researchers, and practitioners can self-host the full stack — Qdrant, Ollama, the harvester pipeline, and the AUX.IO page generator. See the GitHub repository to get started.
