Hi [Name / Team],

I'm Rob, co-founder of ARCHAI — a cultural AI platform that makes museum collections conversational and street-deployable.

We've been building something different: every object in our archive generates a living page — visitor can scan a QR code, ask questions, hear stories, dig into provenance. The page loads anywhere, works offline-first, and links back to the source institution's full record.

We just launched **downloadable posters** — CC0, printable at A4 / A2 / A0 / sticker / postcard sizes, with IIIF museum images, QR codes, and full attribution baked in. They're designed to be pasted on walls, given out at events, or used as stickers.

We're running an open call for street artists, collectives, and public art organisations to take ARCHAI posters into the wild — walls, zines, exhibitions, interventions. We want to see archive objects next to new work, in unexpected places, starting conversations that museums can't start themselves.

No charge, no strings, no account needed. Download and go.
