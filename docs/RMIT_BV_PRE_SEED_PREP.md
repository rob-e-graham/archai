# ARCHAI RMIT-Breakthrough Victoria Pre-Seed Preparation

Last updated: 2026-06-29

This note translates Dr Eric Hoefgen's RMIT-Breakthrough Victoria Pre-Seed Fund guidance into an ARCHAI preparation checklist. It is not legal, investment, or IP advice; it is a working document for discussion with RMIT Research Partnerships and Translation, IP&C, and potential GLAM partners.

## Key Interpretation

The RMIT-BV Pre-Seed Fund is an investment pathway, not a research grant. For ARCHAI to be considered, the opportunity needs to be framed as a venture or commercialisation pathway using or licensing RMIT IP, with evidence of technical readiness, market need, impact, and commercial/social return.

## ARCHAI Fit

- Technology area: sovereign semantic interface layer for GLAM institutions.
- Research context: Rob Graham's RMIT PhD research, developed through FAMTEC as a practice-based research prototype.
- Impact claim: improves staff and visitor access to collection knowledge while keeping institutional data, rights, and governance in-house.
- Social impact angle: accessibility, regional/smaller institution capacity, multilingual access, cultural safety, and open GLAM tooling.
- Venture pathway: local deployment, implementation support, training, connector development, and partner pilots rather than cloud lock-in.

## Eligibility Questions To Resolve

1. IP Position
- What parts of ARCHAI are RMIT IP, Rob/FAMTEC pre-existing IP, third-party open-source components, and institution/source data?
- Should a Notification of Disclosure (NoD) be submitted for the semantic interface layer, Obtext method, AUXIO workflow, or toolkit architecture?
- What licence or assignment model would allow open-source toolkit goals while still supporting investment?

2. Technology Readiness
- Current estimate: TRL 3-4.
- Evidence: working local prototype, Qdrant/Ollama pipeline, multiple harvesters, public website demo, generated AUXIO pages, voice/browser demo, rights-aware media gates, and prototype staff workflows.
- Gap: formal technical validation and repeatable installation/deployment scripts for partner environments.

3. Market Validation
- Needed: discovery calls and written feedback from museums, galleries, archives, CMS/DAMS vendors, universities, and public-program teams.
- Needed: partner letters of interest for institutional pilots.
- Useful programs: ON Prime, RMIT Activator, RP&T commercialisation sessions.

4. Commercial Case
- Possible customers: GLAM institutions, artist-run spaces, university collections, public-art programs, local councils, archives, and digital preservation labs.
- Possible partners: CMS/DAMS providers, IIIF and digital preservation communities, accessibility researchers, public programs teams, and cultural-technology consultants.
- Possible model: deployment package, implementation services, training, support, connector development, and research/pilot funding.

## Lean Canvas Approach

The Lean Canvas article Rob supplied is useful because it argues there is no universal fill order. For ARCHAI, the first canvas should preserve the actual idea backstory, then test the riskiest assumptions first.

For ARCHAI, the riskiest assumptions appear to be:

- Institutions want a semantic interface above existing CMS/DAMS rather than another replacement platform.
- Institutions will trust a local AI layer if source records, legal status, and epistemic limits remain visible.
- GLAM staff will adopt the staff-facing workflows for search, object review, projects, and AUXIO publishing.
- The IP/licensing pathway can support both open-source toolkit ambitions and RMIT-BV investment requirements.
- The product can be installed, maintained, and supported with a simple enough hardware/software stack.
