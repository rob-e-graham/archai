# ARCHAI Partner and Funding Prospect Map

Date: 2026-06-08
Author: Rob Graham / FAMTEC / RMIT

## Purpose

ARCHAI should begin building a serious partner network for research, pilot testing, funding, and long-term open-source development. This document maps likely collaborators and explains why they matter.

The goal is not to send everyone the same email. Different organisations should receive different asks:

- source institutions: transparency, rights review, correction path, feedback
- GLAM pilot partners: testing, staff workflows, visitor evaluation, accessibility trials
- university partners: research design, ethics, grants, evaluation, publications
- technical partners: open-source infrastructure, speech, preservation, CMS/DAMS integration
- peak bodies: sector legitimacy, introductions, dissemination
- funders: research impact, public value, regional access, sovereign infrastructure

## Core Proposition For Partners

ARCHAI is a working, open-source, local-first research toolkit for cultural institutions. It already supports meaningful experiments in semantic search, staff-facing collection intelligence, AUX.IO visitor pages, rights-aware discovery, multilingual interpretation, and local AI. The partnership opportunity is to tailor these capabilities to real institutional needs, evaluate their effect on staff and visitors, and turn the prototype into a governed, reusable GLAM toolkit without handing institutional knowledge or visitor data to opaque cloud systems.

Potential partner language:

> We are looking for GLAM, research, accessibility, and technology partners to help co-design, develop, test, evaluate, and launch ARCHAI as a rights-aware, locally hosted, open-source toolkit for collection discovery, staff workflows, and visitor access. A bounded implementation can begin now; suitable partnerships may later support an RMIT-led ARC Linkage-style proposal.

## ARC-Style Funding Shape

ARCHAI could fit a future Australian Research Council style bid if framed around a clear research problem, not just software development.

Possible high-level framing:

> How can local, open-source AI infrastructure support trustworthy, rights-aware, accessible, and culturally responsible interaction with distributed museum and gallery collections?

Likely ARC-aligned themes:

- cultural heritage infrastructure
- sovereign / local AI
- public access to collections
- accessibility and inclusive design
- regional and small-museum capacity
- AI governance and institutional trust
- born-digital preservation
- multilingual and cross-cultural interpretation
- human-computer interaction in GLAM
- ethical data reuse and rights-aware publishing

Funding note:

ARC scheme details, deadlines, eligibility, and partner contribution rules change and must be checked before any application. Treat this document as a prospect map, not funding advice.

## Potential Research Questions

1. How can local AI systems provide conversational access to museum collections while preserving institutional authority and rights metadata?
2. What staff workflows are needed for curators, collections managers, educators, and technicians to trust AI-assisted collection interpretation?
3. How does AUX.IO change visitor engagement when object pages can respond, speak, listen, and preserve feedback?
4. How can semantic search support cross-institutional discovery without centralising ownership of cultural data?
5. What governance model is needed for open-source AI tools in culturally sensitive GLAM contexts?
6. How can browser and local speech tools improve accessibility without creating new privacy risks?
7. What data structures are needed to separate canonical records, interpretive layers, visitor contributions, and generated outputs?
