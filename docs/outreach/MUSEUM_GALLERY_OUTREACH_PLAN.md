# ARCHAI Museum & Gallery Outreach Plan

Date: 2026-06-08
Author: Rob Graham / FAMTEC / RMIT

## Purpose

ARCHAI now uses open or publicly accessible collection data from multiple museums and aggregators. The next ethical and strategic step is to contact the institutions whose collection records are represented in the demo, explain the research clearly, invite feedback, and open the door to pilot partnerships.

This outreach should be transparent, respectful, and non-extractive. The tone is:

> We are using open/public collection data under its stated rights for doctoral research and open-source software development. We have preserved attribution, source links, and item-level legal status. We would value your feedback and would welcome future collaboration if the work aligns with your institution.

## Outreach Goals

1. Identify research partners who can help co-design, develop, test, evaluate, and launch ARCHAI as an open-source GLAM toolkit through the RMIT Design PhD.
2. Invite bounded implementation experiments that can create useful staff and visitor outcomes with the working prototype now.
3. Build relationships and evidence for future collaborative funding, including a potential RMIT-led ARC Linkage-style pathway.
4. Notify source institutions that ARCHAI has used their open/public collection data in a research prototype.
5. Make the legal posture explicit: source rights remain attached, restricted material is gated out of public demo surfaces, and object views show legal status.
6. Invite corrections if any record, image, attribution, rights statement, or framing needs adjustment.
7. Build goodwill with the open GLAM and open-source collections community.

## What To Include

Every first-contact email should include:

- one-sentence ARCHAI description
- the research context: PhD research at RMIT, developed independently through FAMTEC
- the source-data context: open/public collection data, rights preserved, source links retained
- the public demo context: working prototype, not a finished product
- the legal/correction invitation: happy to correct, remove, relabel, or adjust any object if needed
- the collaboration invitation: feedback, pilot testing, accessibility review, data workflow discussion, grant or research partnership
- links to project page, GitHub, and paper

## Important Wording

Use:

- "open/public collection data"
- "item-level rights preserved"
- "source institution remains the authority"
- "research prototype"
- "rights-aware public demo"
- "we welcome corrections"
- "we would value your feedback"

Avoid:

- "scraped"
- "trained on your data"
- "we added your collection to our app" without context
- "AI interpretation" without grounding or rights explanation
- any wording that implies endorsement

## Legal / Ethical Baseline

ARCHAI should state clearly:

- Third-party collection records, media, trademarks, and institutional names remain the property of their respective owners.
- Use of open/public data in ARCHAI does not imply endorsement by the source institution.
- ARCHAI preserves source links, identifiers, rights labels, and item-level licence detail wherever available.
- Public demo surfaces are intended to display only media that passes the current legal gate.
- If an institution identifies an issue, ARCHAI will correct, relabel, remove, or restrict the affected record promptly.

## Suggested Sequence

1. Prepare one short, respectful email using `SOURCE_INSTITUTION_EMAIL_TEMPLATE.md`.
2. Send individually, not as a bulk blast.
3. Personalise the first paragraph for each institution.
4. Record each send in `OUTREACH_TRACKER.md`.
5. Follow up after 10-14 days only if there is a clear reason.
6. Log all feedback, corrections, and partnership signals.
7. Reflect important feedback back into README, NOTICE, legal gates, and the app UI.

## Preferred Ask

Keep the first commitment small, but make the research purpose clear:

> Would someone on your digital collections, research, interpretation, accessibility, or curatorial technology team be open to a short discussion about shaping or testing ARCHAI as an open-source GLAM toolkit? A first step could be a technical review, staff workflow conversation, or bounded collection experiment.

Do not ask for an immediate financial commitment. Explain that suitable partnerships may later support a collaboratively developed RMIT/ARC-style proposal through cash or in-kind contributions, but begin with shared research questions and a useful experiment.

## Success Signals

- They acknowledge the work and do not object.
- They provide corrections or improved rights guidance.
- They share the project internally.
- They ask for a demo.
- They introduce a digital collections, rights, education, accessibility, or innovation contact.
- They invite a pilot conversation.

## Risks

- An institution may object to representation even when the data is open.
- An item may carry more complex rights than the API field suggests.
- Aggregator records may point to partner institutions with different expectations.
- Some public-domain/open-access material may still require culturally sensitive handling.

Mitigation:

- Contact institutions early.
- Keep legal status visible.
- Keep takedown/correction path simple.
- Preserve source authority in all language.
- Avoid implying endorsement.
