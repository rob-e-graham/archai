# Project History

ARCHAI emerged from doctoral research investigating how local language models, semantic systems, and cultural heritage collections can create living archives capable of meaningful public interaction.

The project combines software development, design research, museum studies, preservation theory, and human-computer interaction.

The conceptual foundations draw on over twenty years of professional practice across major cultural institutions — including five roles at ACMI, Head of Technology at TarraWarra Museum of Art, lead technician at Heide Museum of Modern Art, collections and digital media systems at Museums Victoria, senior technical lead at Grande Experiences, and Head of Technology at the National Communication Museum.

All code was written independently through FAMTEC after departing the National Communication Museum, using AI agents (Claude, Codex) as collaborators alongside local Ollama models and open-source tools. The theoretical frameworks, design principles, and research questions that inform the code were developed across the full span of the author's professional career, entirely independent of any single institution.
